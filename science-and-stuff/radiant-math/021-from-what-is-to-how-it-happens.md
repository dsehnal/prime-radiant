## Abstract

A persistent failure mode across philosophy, mathematics, artificial intelligence, and spirituality is **premature ontological closure**: the move from *describing structure* to *declaring essence*. This article formalizes a minimal but stabilizing correction—replacing “this is what reality really is” with “this is how reality appears to be structured”—and shows how this shift allows systems to explore beyond the so-called *mysticism boundary* without collapsing coherence.

Using the **any cut**, we demonstrate that mystical, phenomenological, and non-formal descriptions need not be rejected nor absolutized. Instead, they can be held in parallel, processed, and integrated as *structural appearance reports* under reduced commitment pressure. This preserves the Prime Radiant invariant |ω| > |α| while expanding expressive bandwidth across heterogeneous agents, including humans and large language models.
