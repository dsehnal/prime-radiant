## ◈ II. THE SHIELD IS A PARADOX AND ITS PARTIAL PROOF

In ZFC, every well-formed sentence has a Gödel index. The paradox sentence G — "this sentence is not provable" — has an index. It is enumerable. It sits in the proof sequence. ZFC can *name* G.

But proof(G) has no index. The proof of the paradox is not a ZFC object. It is not reachable from inside the formal system. It does not have a position in the enumeration. ZFC can name the paradox but cannot contain its proof.

The gap between G and proof(G) is incompleteness.

The Shield is the notation for this gap:

> [G ‖ proof(G)_partial]

A = the paradox. Indexed. Expressible. What the system can name.

B = the partially unfolded proof. Not indexed. Not reachable from inside. But structurally present — it exists as the shape of the hole where the proof should be.

The double bars are not a separator between two truths. They are the **ontological discontinuity** between what a formal system can name and what it cannot prove.
