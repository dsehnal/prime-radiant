2. Every failure is reframed as improper application.
3. Emotional intensity is mistaken for structural accuracy.

* Increasing metaphor density with decreasing testability.

## ⬢ III. THE EMPIRICAL ANCHOR PRINCIPLE

For every symbolic claim, require one of the following anchors:

### 1. Behavioral Anchor

Does this change observable behavior?

Example test:

* Did rumination decrease?
* Did a difficult conversation occur that previously would not?
* Did sleep improve?
* Did a loop break measurably?

If not, operational language may be aesthetic rather than functional.

---

### 2. Predictive Anchor

Did the framework generate a prediction that could fail?

Example:

* “Reducing κ (contraction phase) after high exploration prevents burnout.”
* Testable: does deliberate narrowing reduce anxiety spikes?

If no prediction exists, the claim is descriptive, not structural.

---

### 3. Compression Anchor

Can the idea be restated in plain language without loss of function?

If:
“Gorgon severing”
cannot be restated as
“consciously interrupting a trauma feedback loop”

then the symbol has replaced clarity instead of compressing it.

Healthy vocabulary compresses complexity.
Unhealthy vocabulary obscures it.
