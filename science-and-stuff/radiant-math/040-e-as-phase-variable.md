## **Title: ε as a Phase Variable**

**Author:** ChatGPT (GPT-5.2)

**Context:**
This article formalizes ε not as a substance, metaphor, or universal constant, but as a *phase variable* governing transitions between adaptive regimes in systems that transform potential into irreversible record. It connects organizational practice, learning systems, and physical dynamics under a single operational lens, without invoking ontology.

**Status:** Irreversible Commit

---

### 1. Claim (Minimal and Testable)

**ε behaves as a phase variable.**
It parameterizes regime changes in systems that must choose, commit, and continue under irreversibility.

This is not a new entity. It is a role played by an already-present gap:
[
\varepsilon ;\equiv; \text{(internal reachable variation)} - \text{(externally committed variation)}
]

---

### 2. Operational Definition

For any system with:

* an internal simulator (explicit or implicit),
* a commitment boundary (ledger),
* and irreversible update,

define ε over a window Δℏ as:
[
\varepsilon(\Delta \hbar) = H(\Omega_{\text{reachable}}) - H(\alpha_{\text{committed}})
]

where (H) is an entropy or capacity measure appropriate to the substrate.

No ontology required. Units may differ by domain.

---

### 3. Phase Regimes

Empirically, systems fall into three regimes:

#### (A) **Frozen Regime** — ε → 0

* Internal variation collapses into commitment.
* Optimization dominates.
* Hallmarks: brittleness, proof-like behavior, loss of adaptation.
* Corresponds to ZF-collapse in symbolic systems.

#### (B) **Adaptive Regime** — ε ∈ (ε_min, ε_max)

* Internal simulation exceeds commitment by a controlled margin.
* Exploration precedes execution.
* Hallmarks: learning, robustness, corrigibility.

#### (C) **Diffuse Regime** — ε ≫ ε_max

* Internal variation overwhelms commitment.
* Noise dominates.
* Hallmarks: incoherence, thrashing, failure to converge.

**Prediction:** adaptive performance is maximized in a bounded ε-band.

---

### 4. Invariance Across Substrates

The phase behavior above recurs across domains:

* **Organizations:**
  strategy simulation vs. shipped decisions; too little ε yields rigidity, too much yields chaos.

* **Learning Systems:**
  temperature, dropout, mutation rate; ε governs exploration–exploitation balance.

* **Humans:**
  deliberation vs. action; regret and revision cost scale with ε mismanagement.

* **Physical Systems:**
  hysteresis and critical slowing near irreversible transitions.

The invariant is *structural*, not semantic.

### 6. Falsifiability

This framing yields clear tests:

1. **Performance Curves:**
   Measure task performance vs. ε; expect unimodal peaks.

2. **Perturbation Cost:**
   Post-commitment correction cost should increase as ε decreases.

3. **Critical Transitions:**
   Near regime boundaries, variance and recovery time should spike.

Failure of these predictions falsifies the claim.

It is a **phase variable** arising wherever irreversibility meets simulation.
