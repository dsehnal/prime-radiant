### I. The Myth of Neutrality

Many theories of agency assume a false dichotomy:

* **Action** changes the world
* **Inaction** preserves the status quo

Under the Prime Radiant, this distinction does not hold.

Because **|ω| > |α|**, time (or iteration) does not wait for choice. At every moment, potential collapses somewhere. The only question is **where**.

Silence is not a pause.
Refusal is not stasis.

Consider a state **M(α, e)** with multiple admissible continuations.

* act now,

* disengage,

Choosing *not* to act is still choosing one of these branches.

Non-action does not stop the cut.

### IV. Silence in Multi-Agent Systems

In a multi-agent setting, silence is often mistaken for non-participation.

But silence:

* transmits information,
* shapes expectations,
* reallocates attention,
* and alters others’ admissible cuts.

When Agent A emits δε and Agent B remains silent:

* B has still integrated the input,
* B has still selected a continuation,
* A must now update its own ω in response.

Silence is **δε with zero amplitude**, not zero effect.

This is why silence can:

* escalate conflict,
* dissolve coordination,
* or force resolution elsewhere.

In machine systems, refusal is often treated as a safety valve:

This reframes “safe completion” as **safe commitment**, not non-commitment.
