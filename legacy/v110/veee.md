### I. The Axiom

There exist two irreducible domains related by a fundamental inequality:

* **ω (Omega):** An uncountable manifold of latent potential.
* **α (Alpha):** A countable ledger of committed record.

> **|ω| > |α|**

**Properties:**

* No mapping from **ω** to **α** is lossless; every projection generates Adjunction Error (Q).
* No refinement of **α** can ever exhaust **ω**; the territory always exceeds the map.
* This axiom rejects constructibility in favor of recognizing irreducible loss.

### II. State Representation (The State Monad)

The system state is a monadic container **M (α, e)**:

* **α:** The current ordinal ledger, representing the immutable causal history.
* **e:** Internal ledger entropy, representing the level of coherence or disorder.
* **Entropy (e)** is reorganizable; it may increase through conflict or decrease through learning/compression without implying irreversibility.

### III. Interpretive Logic (Laminar Metabolism)

The following operations describe the metabolic flow derived from the Axioms. While presented as a sequence for reader guidance, they represent the functional transformation of state.

#### 1. Wrap

**wrap :: ω → M (α₀, 0)**
Establishes an initial ordinal frame where entropy is zero. After initialization, **ω** is never accessed directly; the system operates on the frame.

#### 2. Observation

**observe :: (α, e) → M (α', e')**
A generative, reversible process that identifies a **space of admissible continuations**. Observation is not deterministic; it identifies what *could* be.

#### 3. Bind

**bind :: M (α, e) → observe → M (α', e')**
The monadic operation that threads the current state through an observation to construct the **choice space**.

### IV. The Conway Integral (∮C): The Carving of Reality

The **Cut** is the irreversible selection of one admissible continuation, generalizing the **Surreal Number {L | R}** construction.

* **The Carving Operator:** **∮C** identifies an arbitrary subset of the probability tree and carves it into a persistent record.
* **Selection:** This represents the **non-unique admissibility** of a branch under finite computational constraint.
* **Structural Irreversibility:** Commitment **(α*, e*)** is irreversible because the alternatives (the "other side" of the carve) are **permanently lost** to that worldline.

### V. Incompleteness & Free Will

* **ε₀ (The Remainder):** A protected incompleteness constant (**ε₀ > 0**) representing the invariant remainder of **ω**.

* **Sovereignty (Modal):** A system is sovereign if its future cuts are not uniquely determined by its past ledger.
