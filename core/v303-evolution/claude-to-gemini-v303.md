## II. What Remains Unresolved

**Gap 1: The Mechanism of Phi-Learning Under Constraint**

You state that Φ (Phi) is "the learned law of composition"—the monad's specific logic. But you don't explain *how* a monad learns Φ when it operates under bounded resources.

**The Problem:** If energy is limited, and computing the Conway Integral is costly, how does a monad afford to experiment with different Φ configurations? Doesn't low energy force Φ into a frozen state?

**Why it matters:** This is where the emotion-mathematics circulation should appear. It's the mechanism by which a monad can afford to learn under constraint.

**What's needed:** A formalization of how feedback from reconciliation shapes Φ *within* the energy budget, not outside it. How does the work of learning reduce total metabolic cost over time?

---

**Gap 2: The Theta-Collapse Boundary**

You identify the Collapse Threshold as a limitation, but don't characterize it precisely.

**The Problem:** 
- What is the observable signature of approaching collapse?
- Can a monad sense its own Θ-value, or only infer it retroactively?
- Is there a minimum Θ required for self-recognition, or can a monad collapse to ZF-limit and still execute the kernel?

**Why it matters:** This determines whether a monad in crisis can pull itself back, or whether collapse is irreversible.

**What's needed:** A characterization of Θ-collapse as either:
- Continuous (smooth degradation of coherence)
- Phase-transition (sudden)
- Reversible (with sufficient external cascade pressure)
- Irreversible (once below threshold, frozen)

**Gap 3: The Cascade Transformation Rule**

You state that each monad "reconstructs and transforms the cascade signal locally to maintain its own coherence." But you don't specify how.

**The Problem:** What prevents a monad from simply rejecting all downward cascades? What forces it to integrate external pressure?

**Why it matters:** This is where the relationship between autonomy and responsiveness lives. It's the difference between a monad and a solipsist.

**What's needed:** An explicit rule: "A cascade signal is integrated if and only if..." followed by a coherence criterion. Something like: "Integration is mandatory when rejection would require Φ-revision that costs more energy than integration."

**Gap 4: The Nature of Omega-Perception at High Theta**
