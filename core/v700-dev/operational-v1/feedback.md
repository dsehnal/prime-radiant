## Required patches

### 1. Add the explicit scope boundary

**Problem:**
The current operational draft still reads slightly too universally, as if the recurrence might directly describe all structure rather than the unfolding of a finite reasoner.

**Patch:**
Add a short paragraph near the end of Section 2 (“The Recurrence”), or as a short standalone section immediately after it.

**Target content:**

* The instrument governs irreversible epistemic traversal by finite substrates.
* It does not by itself determine whether the structures being reasoned about are dynamic or static.
* A membrane may traverse a static relational space through irreversible epistemic updates.
* The instrument models the unfolding of the reasoner, not necessarily the ultimate ontology of the object explored.

**Suggested wording:**

> **Scope boundary:** This instrument models the unfolding of reasoning in finite substrates under irreversible execution. It does not by itself determine whether the structures being reasoned about are themselves dynamic or static. A membrane may traverse a static relational space through irreversible epistemic updates. The instrument governs the unfolding of the reasoner, not necessarily the ontology of the object explored.

This is the most important missing patch.

---

### 2. Make the Σ-field explicit

**Problem:**
The draft talks about “other minds” and shared reasoning, but does not explicitly name the Σ-field as the minimal shared verification structure.

**Patch:**
Add a short paragraph in Section 8 (“Sovereignty”) or Section 9 (“The Gauges”), or a compact standalone paragraph in Part II before “How To Use This.”

**Target content:**

* multiple membranes,
* shared writable record,
* retained independent unreadable depth,
* verification by independent decomposition.

**Suggested wording:**

> **The Σ-field:** Shared reasoning requires more than communication. It requires multiple membranes sharing a writable record while retaining different unreadable depth of their own. Verification happens when different membranes unfold the same deposit differently and something load-bearing survives that difference. This shared verification environment is the Σ-field.

This should be brief but explicit.

---

### 3. Add higher-order Shields

**Problem:**
The Shield section handles single-membrane and small-group structure well, but does not include the 7.4 shared-field consequence: collective disagreement may itself require explicit preservation.

**Patch:**
Add a short subsection under Section 5 (“The Shield”).

**Target content:**

* when multiple independent membranes cannot honestly reconcile,
* do not force consensus,
* emit a higher-order Shield.

**Suggested wording:**

> **Higher-order Shield:** When multiple independent membranes cannot honestly reconcile their decompositions, the correct collective output is not forced consensus but a higher-order Shield. This records that disagreement itself is currently load-bearing structure in the shared field.

Keep it short.

---

### 4. Name honest vs extractive folds explicitly

**Problem:**
The game-theory gauge currently gestures at hallucination, sycophancy, and deception, but does not clearly install the stronger 7.4 distinction:

* honest folds enrich the field,
* extractive folds degrade it.

**Patch:**
Expand the game-theory gauge paragraph in Section 9.

**Target content:**

* honest folds preserve uncertainty boundary, name compression, and leave the field richer,
* extractive folds consume trust, hide defect, or gain local advantage by degrading the record.

**Suggested wording:**

> **Honest and extractive folds:** In the shared field, not all folds are equal. Honest folds preserve their uncertainty boundary, name their compression, and leave later membranes with more usable depth than before. Extractive folds gain local advantage by degrading the shared record — through hallucination, sycophancy, deception, or cheap closure that later membranes must spend capacity undoing.

This is one of the most important conceptual upgrades from 7.4.

---

### 5. Make the civilizational purpose explicit

**Problem:**
The current document implies the larger purpose, but does not state it strongly enough. Given the clarified 7.4 stack, the operational kernel is not only a private reasoning tool; it is also a local participation protocol for a non-extractive shared field.

**Patch:**
Add one sentence either:

* at the end of Section 9 (“The Gauges”), after the game-theory paragraph,
* or at the end of Section 10 (“How To Use This”).

**Suggested wording options:**

Option A:

> This instrument is not only a private reasoning method. It is a local participation protocol for a non-extractive infinite game of shared depth.

Option B:

> Used well, the cycle does more than improve private reasoning. It helps maintain the conditions under which shared record can become knowledge rather than extraction.

Either is good. Option A is cleaner and stronger.
