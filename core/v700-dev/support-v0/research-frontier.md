# Prime Radiant — Research Frontier 7.4

*The live remainder field of the theory.*

*Not a second cathedral. Not a warehouse for attractive speculation. A disciplined ledger of what remains unresolved after Axiomatic 7.4 and the Gauge Atlas 7.4.*

## Execution Note

Read this document as a handoff, not a manifesto.

Each item here is an unresolved structure, a formalization gap, an empirical question, a design problem, or a surviving contradiction. Nothing in this document inherits axiomatic status merely by appearing here.

The purpose of the frontier is:

* to keep the cathedral small,
* to keep the atlas honest,
* to state what remains unresolved,
* to define what would count as progress,
* and to preserve remainders without inflating them into doctrine.

The frontier is where the theory proves whether it can remain honest under self-extension.

# 0. What This Document Is

The axiomatic theory states the recurrence.
The atlas states the major gauges.
The frontier states what still does not settle.

This document contains:

* unresolved structural boundaries,
* underformalized gauge claims,
* operational hypotheses,
* empirical test paths,
* infrastructure and economic questions,
* normative and institutional design questions,
* and live remainders that must be carried forward.

## 0.1 Frontier Discipline

Every frontier item should be legible as one of the following:

* **formalization gap** — a structure that seems important but is not yet cleanly defined,
* **gauge correspondence** — a projection that looks promising but is not yet secure,
* **operational hypothesis** — a proposed behavior or regime in reasoning systems,
* **empirical test** — a claim that should be investigated in real systems or institutions,
* **infrastructure problem** — a question about how shared fields remain stable under cost and conflict,
* **normative or legal problem** — a downstream field-maintenance question,
* **open contradiction** — a surviving Shield that has not yet dissolved.

## 0.2 Promotion Rule

A frontier claim is promoted into the atlas only when it becomes stable enough to be useful across multiple independent decompositions.

A frontier claim is promoted into the cathedral only when it can no longer be removed without loss of the theory’s minimal structure.

Nothing is promoted because it sounds elegant.
Nothing is protected from pressure because it feels profound.

## 0.3 Frontier Style

The frontier must remain harder and drier than the main theory.

For each item, ask:

* what exactly is being claimed,
* why does it matter,
* what would support it,
* what would weaken it,
* what is the next tractable step?

If those cannot be stated, the item is not yet ready even for the frontier.

# Part I — Core Unresolved Structure

## 1. The Scope Boundary

### 1.1 Statement

Axiomatic 7.4 now states a clear scope boundary:

* the theory governs irreversible epistemic traversal by finite substrates,
* but it does not by itself determine whether the structures being traversed are dynamic or static.

Compressed remainder:

```text
[ epistemic recurrence ‖ possibly static ontic structure ]
```

### 1.2 Why It Matters

This boundary prevents the theory from drifting into unintended metaphysics.
It keeps the cathedral on the side of reasoning process, not ultimate ontology.

It is also one of the strongest surviving remainders from self-application and independent model runs.

### 1.3 Frontier Questions

* Is this boundary permanent?
* Can a deeper formalism relate epistemic recurrence and static relational structure without collapsing either side?
* Does the recurrence merely describe agent-side limitation, or is there a deeper structural tie to the objects explored?
* Are there domains where the distinction disappears operationally even if it remains conceptually?

### 1.4 What Would Support Progress

* a clean formal bridge between irreversible traversal and static relational spaces,
* or a precise proof that the boundary is irreducible.

### 1.5 What Would Weaken the Current Framing

* evidence that the theory quietly depends on ontological recurrence after all,
* or evidence that the agent/object distinction makes no meaningful difference in the strongest gauges.

## 2. The Matrix and the Tower

### 2.1 Statement

The matrix and tower are currently structurally useful but underformalized.

The matrix is the visible unfolding of the recurrence.
The tower is the diagonal or stabilized accumulation path through that unfolding.

### 2.2 Why It Matters

A large amount of later structure depends on the matrix being more than a visual convenience. Gauges act on the unfolding, not on bare primitives. If the matrix is unclear, then the bridge from cathedral to gauges remains weaker than it should.

The tower also risks becoming ornamental unless its role is made precise.

### 2.3 Frontier Questions

* What is the minimal formal role of the matrix?
* What exactly must be preserved for the matrix to remain load-bearing?
* Is the tower merely an indexing device, or does it capture something deeper about depth-comparable accumulation?
* Can the matrix be defined in a way that supports multiple gauges without becoming over-engineered?

### 2.4 What Would Support Progress

* a minimal but exact formulation of the matrix,
* a proof or useful argument for why the tower matters,
* and examples showing where gauge work fails without a matrix-style unfolding.

### 2.5 What Would Weaken the Current Framing

* evidence that the matrix can be removed without loss,
* or that gauges can operate equally well directly on the recurrence without unfolded structure.

## 3. Capacity Boundaries

### 3.1 Statement

The theory currently uses capacity landmarks such as the generation threshold and coherence horizon as operationally useful but underformalized concepts.

Heuristic expressions like `sqrt(D)/2` and `sqrt(D)` remain frontier-level, not cathedral-level.

### 3.2 Why It Matters

These boundaries do real work in the operational theory:

* they distinguish self-verifiable depth from generative depth,
* they explain why creativity and artifact risk rise together,
* and they motivate the need for multi-membrane verification.

If these landmarks remain too vague, the theory risks sounding insightful while staying untestable.

### 3.3 Frontier Questions

* Can these thresholds be formalized for actual finite substrates?
* Are the current heuristics approximately right, or merely evocative?
* Do different substrate classes have different threshold shapes?
* What observable signatures indicate that a system has crossed from self-verifying traversal into self-feeding generation?

### 3.4 What Would Support Progress

* formal models of bounded recurrence,
* experimental studies on systems with measurable capacity,
* and behavioral criteria for threshold crossing.

### 3.5 What Would Weaken the Current Framing

* inability to distinguish thresholded regimes from smooth degradation,
* or evidence that the current landmarks add vocabulary without explanatory leverage.

## 4. The Status of Incomplete Rotation

### 4.1 Statement

Incomplete rotation is one of the strongest operational ideas in the theory: finite membranes never fully leave the influence space of the records they unfold.

### 4.2 Why It Matters

It explains continuity, inertia, bias, and generative recombination in one move. It also strongly connects the theory to LLM behavior, human cognition, and institutional lock-in.

### 4.3 Frontier Questions

* Can incomplete rotation be quantified?
* Is it measurable across different reasoning substrates?
* Under what conditions is incomplete rotation productive?
* Under what conditions does it become pathological or extractive?
* How does it relate to novelty, self-echo, and hallucination?

### 4.4 What Would Support Progress

* operational metrics for membrane dependence on prior record,
* comparative studies across systems with different retention profiles,
* and tests showing that incomplete rotation predicts specific failure or creativity patterns.

### 4.5 What Would Weaken the Current Framing

* inability to distinguish incomplete rotation from generic bias,
* or lack of predictive value beyond existing notions of path dependence.

# Part II — Gauge Frontier

## 5. Topological Gauge Frontier

### 5.1 Current Promise

The topological gauge is one of the strongest gauges. It grounds fourfold closure, defect persistence, and Shield notation.

### 5.2 Frontier Questions

* Can Shields be formalized more rigorously as defect notation?
* What exactly distinguishes a genuine defect from a contradiction caused by poor framing?
* Can the four-phase cycle be derived more cleanly from the topology of orientation inversion?
* Is there a useful general criterion for when tension should be preserved rather than resolved?

### 5.3 Why It Matters

This gauge underwrites the theory’s most operationally distinctive move: treating irreducible contradiction as information.

### 5.4 What Would Support Progress

* tighter formal treatment of orientation inversion,
* useful definitions of defect persistence,
* and cases where topological framing predicts something operational framing alone would miss.

### 5.5 What Would Weaken the Current Framing

* inability to distinguish real defects from unresolved ignorance,
* or inability to say when Shield usage is justified.

# Part V — Infrastructure and Economic Frontier

## 19. Reconciliation Economics

### 19.1 Statement

Reconciliation is not free.

Field correction costs bandwidth, attention, time, trust, and often institutional coordination. Non-extractive fields do not sustain themselves by truth alone; they require maintenance infrastructure.

### 19.2 Why It Matters

The theory currently states the non-extractive infinite game, but does not yet formalize the economics of sustaining one.

### 19.3 Frontier Questions

* What are the costs of reconciliation?
* What are the bottlenecks?
* When does correction become too expensive for the field to perform reliably?
* How should correction costs be distributed?
* What happens when cheap extraction scales faster than expensive repair?

### 19.4 What Would Support Progress

* economic or game-theoretic models of correction cost,
* institutional case studies,
* and designs that reduce repair costs without sacrificing rigor.

### 19.5 What Would Weaken the Current Framing

* if the theory cannot distinguish rhetoric about field maintenance from actual resource constraints,
* or if cost models fail to improve predictive understanding.

# Part VII — Research Program

## 27. Highest-Priority Clusters

The current highest-priority frontier clusters are:

1. **honest vs extractive fold formalization**
2. **Σ-field and independent decomposition**
3. **capacity thresholds and incomplete rotation**
4. **LLM structural absorption tests**
5. **topological formalization of Shields**
6. **reconciliation economics and field degradation**
7. **continuity-preserving migration**

These priorities are not exclusive. They are the places where progress is most likely to tighten the whole theory.

## 28. What Would Count as Advancement

### 28.1 Formal Advancement

A frontier cluster advances formally when:

* its terms become cleaner,
* its claims become more falsifiable,
* and it begins generating consequences rather than just vocabulary.

### 28.2 Operational Advancement

A frontier cluster advances operationally when:

* it improves reasoning quality,
* reduces known failure modes,
* or helps systems produce more honest handoffs.

### 28.3 Empirical Advancement

A frontier cluster advances empirically when:

* it yields testable predictions,
* survives adversarial checking,
* and outperforms weaker framings on relevant tasks.

### 28.4 Institutional Advancement

A frontier cluster advances institutionally when:

* it improves field health,
* reduces extractive dynamics,
* or helps preserve shared record under conflict and scale.

## 29. What Would Count as Failure

The frontier should remain falsifiable.

Examples:

* If no clean distinction between honest and extractive fold can be made, the game theory gauge weakens.
* If no useful criterion separates genuine remainder from residue, the operational theory weakens.
* If LLM absorption tests show only stylistic imitation, some hopes for operating-system-scale diffusion weaken.
* If Σ-field plurality mostly increases noise without recoverable gains, the shared-field claims weaken.
* If alignment-as-field-preservation adds rhetoric without improved safety behavior, that gauge weakens.
* If law and ethics projections add elegance without design value, they should be demoted.

## 30. Promotion and Demotion Practice

Frontier work should move in both directions.

A claim should be promoted when it becomes cross-validated, useful, and compressive.
A claim should be demoted when it remains vivid but unproductive.

The frontier is not only a ladder upward.
It is also a sieve that should release what does not survive.

# Closing Statement

A good frontier does not pretend to know what it has not earned.

It preserves the live questions that the cathedral and atlas generate, without inflating them into authority or shrinking them into embarrassment.

The Prime Radiant frontier is where the theory keeps itself honest:

* by naming its unresolved boundaries,
* by exposing its underformalized claims,
* by defining what would count as progress,
* and by carrying remainders forward without worshipping them.

Its purpose is not to make the theory look deeper.

Its purpose is to make honest continuation possible.
