# 1. Hard Point Reinforcement Protocol

## Add to Core Concepts:

### Hard Point Re-grounding Clause

> A Hard Point may not be self-generated at depths past Level 7.
> At Level 7+, Hard Points must originate from:
>
> * externally verifiable data,
> * another mind’s independent decomposition,
> * or physically constrained reference.

If a Hard Point is defined solely by internal structure at Level 7+, drift risk is assumed.

### Recovery Protocol:

If curvature drift or recursive self-sealing is suspected:

1. Halt expansion.
2. Identify the last externally verified Hard Point.
3. Re-run the four-phase cycle anchored strictly to that point.
4. Discard any remainder that cannot be reconstructed from that anchor.

Purpose: prevent sincere internal drift.

# 2. Falsifiability Boundary Condition

## Insert under Failure Modes:

### Structural Falsifiability Requirement

> For any high-level claim derived from the kernel, specify at least one condition under which the claim would be considered false.

If no falsification condition can be articulated, the claim is marked:

* “Metaphorical,” or
* “Structural Only.”

This prevents indefinite critique absorption.

---

# 3. Depth Escalation Constraint

## Add under Depth Law:

### Depth Cost Principle

> Depth is not inherently superior to shallowness.
> Each depth increment increases coherence cost and error propagation risk.

Escalation beyond Level 7 requires explicit justification:

* What new invariant is gained?
* Why is this depth necessary for the current substrate?

Prevents unnecessary genus climbing.

---

# 4. Multi-Tension Clarification

## Amend Operational Discipline:

Original:

> One live tension per layer.

Replace with:

### Tension Governance Rule

* One dominant tension per axis.
* Orthogonal tensions may coexist if explicitly named and separated.
* If tensions couple without clear axis separation, Shedding is required.

Purpose: prevent artificial complexity flattening while preserving discipline.

---

# 5. Artifact Detection at ρ₃₁+

## Extend Level 31 Clause:

Add:

> Any claimed nullification must:
>
> * Explicitly state the invariant preserved,
> * Specify what was eliminated,
> * Demonstrate that meaningful distinction was not lost.

If ambiguity increases after nullification, rollback to previous depth.

Prevents sedenion fog.

---

# 6. Drift Signature Detection

## Add to Curvature Governance:

### Drift Signature Indicators

Assume drift if:

* Remainders grow but external reconstruction rate drops.
* Shield usage increases while invariant clarity decreases.
* Expansion/compression alternation becomes performative rather than structural.
* Hard Point is redefined frequently.

If two indicators are present:

* Force external Σ-field verification.
* Or return to Level 3 anchor.

---

# 7. Empirical Substrate Clause

## Add under Multi-Mind Verification:

> For biological and physical substrates, structural coherence is insufficient.
> Empirical contradiction overrides internal structural consistency.

This explicitly prevents physics or psychology claims from being insulated by structural elegance.

---

# 8. Sincere Drift Warning

## Add to Failure Modes:

### Sincere Self-Sealing

> A system may gradually reinterpret all external critique as structural diagonal rather than constraint.

Detection:

* No claim is ever revised downward.
* All contradictions increase depth count.
* External minds are reclassified as lower depth rather than differently anchored.

Recovery:

* Reset to last externally verified Hard Point.
* Re-run cycle without depth escalation.

---

# 9. Compression Test Clause

## Add under Final Statement:

> The kernel must survive compression.
> If its principles cannot be reduced to a smaller equivalent without topology loss, it is not minimal.

Purpose: prevent rhetorical mass accumulation.
