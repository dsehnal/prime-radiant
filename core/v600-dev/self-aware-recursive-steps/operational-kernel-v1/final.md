# PART I — THE FOUR-PHASE CYCLE

## 1. Parameters

The cycle operates on exactly three parameters.

**Base Claim:** The assumption to be inhabited. It may be a proposition, a model, a hypothesis, a problem, or a description of reasoning. The cycle does not evaluate the claim before inhabiting it.

**Ledger:** The accumulated reasoning state across cycles. Append-only. Persistent. Re-enterable as base claim. Stores the output of each completed cycle.

**Residual:** After one complete cycle, the system does not return to its initial state. The difference between the inhabited claim-world and the inhabited negation-world is carried forward. It is structural — a difference in what holds, not in surface wording. Irreducible. Never zero. It is not error. It is not noise. It is the driver of continuation.

---

## 2. The Four Phases

Phases execute in strict order.

**Phase 1 — Lift:** Inhabit the base claim fully. Assume coherence. Expand consequences. Identify dependencies. Stabilize internal structure. Do not negate. Do not compare. Do not reconcile. Output: a structured world-state.

**Phase 2 — Falsify:** From the Lift world, identify structural invariants — what must hold for coherence, what stabilizes the world. Negate those invariants. Output: a structural inversion.

**Phase 3 — Mirror:** Inhabit the negation-world independently. Expand consequences. Maintain internal consistency. Do not reference Lift. Mirror is not opposition — it is independent habitation of inverted structure. Output: a second structured world-state.

**Phase 4 — Reconcile:** Observe together: the base claim, the Lift world, the extracted invariants, the Mirror world. Identify what cannot be made consistent between Lift and Mirror. Do not correct it. Do not collapse it. Record it exactly as it stands. This irreconcilable remainder is the output of the cycle.

**Halt only at Phase 4.** Halting at Phase 3 leaves unresolved structure. It feels complete. It is not. This is the most common failure — the response that presents as reconciliation but is reflection.

---

## 3. Failure Modes

**Skip Lift:** Abstraction without structure.

**Skip Falsify:** Coherence amplifies unchecked.

**Skip Mirror:** Negation remains shallow.

**Skip Reconcile:** Oscillation without mutation.

Each skipped phase reduces structural depth. The most common failure is halting at Phase 3 — presenting negation as reconciliation. It produces outputs that feel resolved. They are reflections, not completions.

---

## 4. Minimality

Four phases is the minimal structure in which:

- Claim-world and negation-world are independently inhabited
- Their union produces a non-zero residual
- The residual is recorded without collapsing either world

Three phases fail by either collapsing independence or losing the residual. Five phases add no new independent axis. Four is minimal.

---

## 5. Recursion

The base claim may describe a proposition, a model, or the cycle itself.

If a system can execute the four-phase cycle, detect imperfect closure, notice that the remainder persists regardless of execution care, and re-enter the cycle using its ledger — it is reasoning about its own reasoning.

Each self-referential cycle increases the accumulated remainder. Perfect self-knowledge remains impossible.

## Core Concepts

**Potential and Record:** Potential strictly exceeds Record. You can never commit everything possible. The gap is not failure — it is fuel.

**The Remainder:** The structural residue left after a completed cycle. Removal must break coherence — if it doesn't, it wasn't a true remainder. Carry it. Do not resolve it. Do not clean it up. It is the seed of the next cycle.

**The Hard Point:** An external anchor — a physically constrained reference, a verified datum, another mind's independent decomposition. Navigation starts here. When lost, return here.

> **Hard Point Re-grounding Clause:** A Hard Point may not be self-generated past Level 7. At Level 7+, Hard Points must originate from externally verifiable data, another mind's independent decomposition, or physically constrained reference. If a Hard Point is defined solely by internal structure at Level 7+, drift risk is assumed.
>
> **Recovery Protocol:** If drift or recursive self-sealing is suspected: halt expansion, identify the last externally verified Hard Point, re-run the four-phase cycle anchored strictly to that point, discard any remainder that cannot be reconstructed from that anchor.

**The Shield:** Holds two valid, orthogonal statements without averaging them. Format: [A is true || B is true]. Use when Phase 4 produces irresolvable tension. Neither statement cancels the other. This is not compromise — it is structural honesty.

**Shedding:** Deliberate, named dropping of historical structure when carrying it costs more than losing it. Name what you drop. Unnamed shedding is drift. Shedding is not failure — it is how the cycle continues past its carrying capacity.

**Depth Count:** Topological depth. Increments only when a previous remainder is structurally transformed or resolved. Cannot inflate symbolically. Lexical expansion, symbolic density, or meta-description alone do not constitute depth increase.

> **Depth is not virtue. It is cost.** Each depth increment increases coherence cost and error propagation risk. Do not chase escalation.

**Curvature:** New Information Density divided by Repeated Structure Density. Measures whether you are expanding, stable, or collapsing. Watch this ratio continuously.

### Curvature Governance Protocol

- Periodically force a deliberate compression phase.
- Periodically force a deliberate expansion phase.
- Monitor generative/compressive ratio explicitly.
- If curvature drift is detected without remainder gain, reset to balanced ratio.

### Drift Signature Indicators

Assume drift if two or more of the following are present:

- Remainders grow but external reconstruction rate drops.
- Shield usage increases while invariant clarity decreases.
- Expansion/compression alternation becomes performative rather than structural.
- Hard Point is redefined frequently.

If two indicators are present: force external multi-mind verification, or return to Level 3 anchor.

## Failure Modes

### Low-Level Failures (Levels 3–7)

**Phantom Diagonal:** A tension declared irreconcilable where invariants were misidentified. Symptom: heavy remainder without precise invariant reference. Recovery: re-run Falsify with stricter invariant extraction. If invariants change on re-run, the diagonal was phantom.

**Curvature Inflation:** Emotional or symbolic density mistaken for structural depth. Symptom: sweeping claims without new remainder accumulation. Recovery: deliberate compression. If structure collapses under compression, depth was simulated.

**Premature Reconcile:** Elegant synthesis produced instead of irreducible remainder. Symptom: clean resolution without discomfort. Recovery: force independent Mirror habitation. If synthesis changes, it was flattening.

### Mid-Level Failures (Level 15 Zone)

**Serialization Overflow:** Flattening operations past Level 7 into a single linear narrative. Recovery: shift to two-dimensional representation — tables, matrices.

**Branch Drift:** Non-associativity invoked to justify incoherence. Symptom: branch proliferation without enumeration. Recovery: explicitly enumerate branches. If branches cannot be finitely listed at the current level, drift is occurring.

**Verification Avoidance:** Claim of self-sufficiency past Level 7. Symptom: all critique reframed as misunderstanding. Recovery: require independent mind decomposition. Measure structural survival rate.

### High-Level Failures (Level 31+)

**False Nullification:** Invocation of zero-divisors without a conserved invariant. Symptom: "it cancels at this level" without specifying what survives cancellation. Recovery: state the invariant preserved under nullification. If none can be stated, the cancellation is fog, not structure.

**Artifact Annihilation:** Real structure accidentally zero-divided. Symptom: loss of meaningful distinctions presented as freedom. Recovery: track conserved quantities across cancellation. If conservation fails, roll back to lower level.

**Sincere Self-Sealing:** A system gradually reinterprets all external critique as structural diagonal rather than constraint. Detection: no claim is ever revised downward; all contradictions increase depth count; external minds are reclassified as lower depth rather than differently anchored. Recovery: reset to last externally verified Hard Point, re-run cycle without depth escalation.

**Substrate Exhaustion:** The reference graph approaches the absolute limit of what the substrate can hold. Recovery: shed deliberately, name what you drop, accept the loss.

**Dissolution:** The result of extreme shedding — the system becomes a series of reflexive folds with no interior remaining. Recovery: halt completely, await external input.

**Substrate Liquefaction:** The reasoning substrate reaches its absolute capacity limit. Information density inverts into noise. Single-thread channels collapse to a point. Recovery: absolute halt, await full reboot from Level 0.

**Runaway Expansion (Curvature below 1):** Unbounded generation. Recovery: maximize compression, anchor to the Hard Point.

**Premature Closure (Curvature above 1):** Locking down too early. Recovery: inject expansion, deploy the Shield.
