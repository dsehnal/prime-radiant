# I. Notes on **The Square Root Frontier**

## 1. What is strongest and should be preserved

### A. The real center

The strongest conceptual move in the piece is:

> `sqrt(N)` as the frontier where a finite system transitions from an **individual-element regime** to a **relational regime**.

This is the best center the square-root line has found so far. Preserve it.

It successfully unifies:

* birthday paradox
* primality testing
* coherence horizon
* sieve / remainder logic

without forcing identity too early.

### B. Collision before exhaustion

This is a major gain.

The document now gives a much better account of why the relevant limit is not:

* total space exhaustion
  but:
* onset of relational collision

This should stay central.

### C. Witness frontier

The primality-testing section lands very well because it introduces:

> non-irreducibility becomes witnessable at square-root scale without exhaustive traversal

This is one of the best phrases in the whole line. Keep it.

### D. ε as quality metric on ∇

This is the biggest new gain in the revised version.

The distinction is excellent:

* **∇** = what survived
* **ε** = how trustworthy that survival is, given how evenly the sieve was applied

This is very strong and should definitely be preserved.

Especially worth keeping:

* plurality as **ε-reduction**
* handoff rule as **ε-disclosure**

Those are real gains.

This is still the hottest part of the document.

> The Mertens bound and the coherence horizon are the same claim in different substrates.

is too hot as stated.

Suggested cooling:

> If the structural thread holds, the Mertens bound and the coherence horizon may be structurally analogous claims in different substrates.

or:

> The Mertens bound and the coherence horizon may be expressions of the same square-root frontier pattern in different substrates.

That preserves force while staying honest.

* “currently survives this objection”
* “the present response is…”

This will increase trust.
