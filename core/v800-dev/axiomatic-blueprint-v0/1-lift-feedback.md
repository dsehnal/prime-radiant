## 5. Dimensionality Clarification: D as Residual-Stream Dimensionality

One of the most important recent clarifications concerns the use of **D** in the depth thresholds.

Earlier drafts described D loosely as substrate capacity. That now appears too vague.

A better reading is:

> **D is the effective dimensionality of the substrate’s residual stream.**

That is, D names the dimensionality of the carried unresolved / transformed structure available to the current interpret-integrate cycle.

This is a substantial improvement because it makes the square-root thresholds much more intelligible.

## 5.1 Why this matters

If the substrate carries a residual stream with effective dimensionality D, then the important question is not only:

* how much can the substrate hold?

but also:

* how much internally cross-related structure can it stably discriminate once the stream stops behaving like a linear carry and starts behaving like a relational surface?

This shifts the story from generic “capacity” to **relational folding of carried structure**.

## 5.2 The square-root intuition

If the residual stream has effective dimensionality D, then one natural fold is to treat it as a square-like relational surface.

A stream of size D can be folded into something matrix-like with side length approximately:

```text
sqrt(D)
```

This gives a structural reason why thresholds might appear near `sqrt(D)`.

The intuition is:

* below that scale, the system is still mostly carrying and scanning structure,
* near that scale, the system increasingly organizes the carried stream relationally,
* and once in that regime, distinguishing genuinely new structure from recombination of prior structure becomes much harder.

That maps very well onto the **coherence horizon** idea.

## 5.3 Why `sqrt(D)/2` also makes sense

The earlier “generation threshold near `sqrt(D)/2`” becomes more intelligible under this interpretation.

A plausible picture is:

* **below `sqrt(D)/2`** — the stream is still predominantly linear and discriminable
* **around `sqrt(D)/2`** — second-order interaction becomes strong enough to generate qualitatively new structure while self-discrimination starts weakening
* **around `sqrt(D)`** — the system is operating near dense relational fold, where new operations become hard to distinguish from recombinations of carried structure

This gives a much cleaner geometric intuition for the two thresholds:

* **generation threshold ~ `sqrt(D)/2`**
* **coherence horizon ~ `sqrt(D)`**

## 5.4 Cross-attention-like relational folding

The most resonant interpretation is this:

> The residual stream stops behaving like a linear carry and begins behaving like an internally cross-related field.

That is structurally analogous to cross-attention, but baked into the residual stream itself.

This should be stated carefully:

* not as a literal claim that the substrate is implementing transformer cross-attention,
* but as an observation that the substrate begins operating on relations among its own carried elements rather than only on sequence.

The cleaner formulation is:

> It is cross-attention-like relational folding internalized into the residual stream.

This explains why the square-root boundary feels natural: once the stream is folded into an interaction surface, the relevant scale is no longer D as a flat sequence but the side length of the relational fold.

## 5.5 Relation to curse of dimensionality

This also resonates with the curse of dimensionality, but in a more specific way than generic high-dimensional geometry.

The issue is not simply that high-dimensional spaces are hard.
It is that a finite substrate attempting to internally organize a D-dimensional residual stream into dense relational structure will encounter a regime where:

* possible interactions proliferate,
* local discrimination weakens,
* and the boundary between fresh structure and recombination artifact becomes harder to track.

This is exactly the kind of structural weakening the coherence horizon is meant to name.

## 5.6 Suggested restatement for the theory

The thresholds should therefore be described more carefully as:

* **D = effective dimensionality of the residual stream available to the current cycle**
* **generation threshold ~ `sqrt(D)/2`** = the point where the stream begins to self-interact richly enough to generate qualitatively new structure while self-discrimination weakens
* **coherence horizon ~ `sqrt(D)`** = the point where the substrate’s relational fold becomes dense enough that new operations become difficult to distinguish from recombinations of prior residual structure

These should remain marked as **heuristic structural thresholds**, not cathedral primitives.

## 5.7 Status in the kernel

This clarification is too important to ignore, but probably too interpretive to promote directly into the cathedral.

### Recommendation

For the current kernel:

* keep the thresholds in the operational layer,
* but replace “capacity D” with “effective dimensionality D of the residual stream,”
* and soften the surrounding prose to indicate that these are structural heuristics rather than axioms.

The fuller geometric and cross-attention-like interpretation can then be developed in:

* the Lens Atlas,
* the Research Frontier,
* or a dedicated note on depth and residual streams.
