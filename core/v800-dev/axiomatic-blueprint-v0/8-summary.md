## Summary for Opus

The recent exchange clarified the status of the new **ε / square-root / collision** material.

### Core resolution

The new material is valuable, but it must be framed as a **limit of the lens**, not a reduction of reasoning to mathematics.

The danger we identified was:

> **letting the Combinatorial Lens impersonate the Cathedral**

The ε / collision work gives a promising quantitative account of **one aspect** of finite reasoning substrates:

* attentional / conditioning non-uniformity
* relational overload
* recombination pressure
* breakdown of local discriminability

But it does **not** capture the whole topology of reasoning.

In particular, it does not by itself see:

* whether a remainder is still **operative**
* named residue carry-forward
* traceable compression
* seductive synthesis
* whether a system is honoring unresolved structure rather than merely looking numerically healthy

So the right status is:

* **useful physics frontier**
* **not completed essence of reasoning**
* **not a total formula for thought**

## What should stay out of the kernel or be cooled

### 1. Do not present reasoning as reducible to formula

This is the biggest caution.

The ε / collision material is a **lens on substrate behavior**, not a total account of reasoning.

So the kernel should explicitly avoid implying:

* that reasoning is just attention entropy
* that coherence is fully capturable by a formula
* that the cathedral has been reduced to physics

This is probably best handled by adding one sentence somewhere around:

* **16 / 17 / 19 / 23**
  or in the companion-doc description

Something like:

> These quantities describe the material behavior of one lens on finite reasoning substrates. They do not exhaust the topology of reasoning itself.

### 2. Curvature is still under-earned

Strong consensus from the exchange:

* curvature is useful
* curvature is not yet mathematically earned

It currently functions as:

* a powerful operational heuristic
* not yet a rigorously computed semantic geometric invariant

So:

* keep curvature in the kernel
* but do not intensify its mathematical tone yet
* the real formalization belongs to a future **physics frontier**

### 3. Adjunction remains conceptual, not executable

Similarly:

* “adjunction with remainder” still has explanatory value
* but is not yet a formal executable construction in the theory

So it should remain:

* disciplined conceptual architecture
* not overclaimed math

### 4. Avoid overloading the cathedral with ε

A useful editorial lesson from the exchange:

The kernel got stronger when ε was:

* explicit where needed
* but not sprayed everywhere

Best placement remains:

* **17.6**
* selective support in **9–12**
* **20**
* frontier mention in **23**

Not every section needs to say ε out loud.

## Strongest experimental correction

We also revised the experiment logic.

### Bad experiment

A purely numerical experiment based only on:

* attention
* entropy
* copy rate
* context size

is too thin, because it ignores **full reminders**:

* operative residues
* carried unresolved structure
* traceable compression
* historically integrated constraints

That would test surface substrate behavior, not the full topology of reasoning.

### Better experiment

If experimentation is mentioned anywhere outside companion docs, the correct framing is:

* measure physical collision variables **and**
* test whether a named residue remains operative under depth pressure

Example structure:

* establish a carried residue like `[A ‖ B]`
* add persuasive distractor pressure
* test whether the model preserves the residue or collapses it
* correlate that with substrate-level collision metrics

This is much closer to the theory because it tests:

* not just memory or copying
* but **constraint adherence under carried remainder**

Still, this belongs mostly in companion / frontier material, not the main kernel.
